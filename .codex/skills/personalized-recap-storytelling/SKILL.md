# Personalized Recap Storytelling

Use this skill for recap products, not generic dashboards.

Apply it when the user is:
- designing a wrapped-style experience
- choosing which metrics become story beats
- turning behavior into identity or archetype cards
- deciding who is eligible for which stories
- planning hubs, revisit surfaces, or share artifacts
- reviewing a recap for trust, cringe, or shareability

Do not use it for:
- generic analytics pages with no narrative intent
- purely visual polish without recap product decisions
- route-specific layout fixes with no story change

## Workflow

1. Define the recap frame.
Read `references/pillars.md` and `references/protagonist-and-segmentation.md`.
Decide who the protagonist is, what moment this recap marks, and whether it is annual, seasonal, milestone-based, or always-on.

2. Build the story run.
Read `references/beat-taxonomy.md` and `references/eligibility-and-fallbacks.md`.
Pick the beat types, the order, the gating rules, and the sparse-data fallback route.

3. Establish the trust contract.
Read `references/trust-and-methodology.md`.
For each beat, define what data counts, what is filtered out, what reference class is used, and how surprises are explained.

4. Design sharing and persistence.
Read `references/social-privacy-and-sharing.md` and `references/control-hub-afterlife.md`.
Decide the primary share artifact, the channel adaptations, the revisit surface, and what persists after the linear run.

5. Write and prune.
Read `references/editorial-rules.md`.
Turn the system into copy and identity packaging without slipping into product chest-thumping or fake intimacy.

6. Evaluate before shipping.
Read `references/evaluation-rubric.md`.
Score the recap for memorability, trust, clarity, novelty, restraint, and share intent. Cut weak beats.

## Default Output Shape

When using this skill, prefer to answer in this order:
- `Story frame`: protagonist, cadence, emotional job
- `Beat plan`: 5 to 9 beats with purpose and ordering
- `Eligibility`: thresholds, fallbacks, and route-outs
- `Trust contract`: methodology, filters, reference class, disputed-result recovery
- `Share system`: primary share artifact, channel adaptations, revisit surface, afterlife
- `Editorial notes`: voice, archetype rules, anti-cringe checks
- `Risks`: what still needs product judgment or user testing

## Rules

- The user is the protagonist. The product is the stagehand.
- Every beat needs a reason to exist beyond "we track this metric."
- Simple broad beats come first. Deeper or weirder beats are earned.
- Identity cards must describe behavior, not essence.
- A recap is not a raw record of all activity. It is a curated reflection with a clear methodology.
- Social comparison requires explicit consent, not a hidden default.
- Sparse-data users need a graceful story, not a broken one.
- Repetition kills delight. Each beat should add a new lens.
- Do not cargo-cult Spotify's look. Reuse the system logic, not the skin.
