# Social Privacy And Sharing

Treat sharing as a product surface, not a share button tacked on at the end.

## Primary Share Artifact

Choose one main share object:
- a single card
- a short stitched sequence
- a comparison result
- a generated summary

It should work even when stripped of the rest of the run.

## Channel Map

For each channel, define:
- what gets shared
- how much context survives
- whether the artifact is static, animated, or interactive
- whether attribution or branding remains visible

## Privacy And Consent

Any shared or social mode needs clear answers for:
- who can see what
- whether others can reshare it
- whether the mode is saved or ephemeral
- what profile data is exposed

Do not default users into public comparison without consent.

## What Makes A Good Shared Artifact

- immediate meaning
- low explanation burden
- identity or flex value
- visually distinct
- not embarrassing in edge cases

## Bad Sharing Patterns

- requiring the whole deck to understand the post
- posting weak metrics because they are available
- hidden social exposure
- an artifact that makes the user look worse than they feel
