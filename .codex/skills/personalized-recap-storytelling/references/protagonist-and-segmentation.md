# Protagonist And Segmentation

Pick the protagonist before you pick the beats.

## Protagonist Types

- `Individual user`
- `Team or workspace`
- `Creator or artist`
- `Operator or developer`
- `Advertiser or brand`

Each protagonist changes what counts as a flex, what counts as trust, and what feels invasive.

## Motivation Segments

Most recap audiences want one or two of these:
- `Flex`: show status, mastery, or commitment
- `Nostalgia`: relive a period or season
- `Self-recognition`: see a true version of themselves
- `Belonging`: feel part of a group or role
- `Curiosity`: learn something surprising

Do not design a run that tries to maximize all of them equally.

## Data-Richness Tiers

- `Zero`: not enough data for a real recap
- `Sparse`: enough for a short reflection, not a full deck
- `Standard`: enough for a full run
- `Rich`: enough for niche or deep beats
- `Power`: enough for advanced, cohort-based, or comparative beats

Use tiers to decide which beats unlock and which route the user sees.

## Practical Rule

For v1, choose:
- one primary protagonist
- one primary motivation
- one default richness tier

Everything else should be treated as a fallback or later extension.
