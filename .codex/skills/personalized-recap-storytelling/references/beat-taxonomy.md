# Beat Taxonomy

Use this to assemble a recap run. Do not use every beat type.

## Core Beat Types

- `Stat card`: A broad anchor metric. Good early in the run.
- `Identity card`: Turns behavior into an interpretable label or role.
- `Evolution card`: Shows change over time, phase shifts, or rank shifts.
- `Remarkable moment`: Surfaces one standout day, streak, or anomaly.
- `Workflow or habit card`: Reveals how the user works, not just what they did.
- `Proof-of-work card`: Shows outcome, quality, delivery, or difficulty.
- `Comparison card`: Compares against peers, cohorts, or friends. Use carefully.
- `Clip or message`: Inserts a human or creator voice into the run.
- `Quiz or game`: Lets the user guess, reveal, or play with the data.
- `Outro or share card`: Compresses the recap into a public artifact.
- `Hub artifact`: A persistent object after the run, such as a playlist or saved report.

## Beat Scoring

Before keeping a beat, score it on:
- `Novelty`: does it add a new lens?
- `Self-recognition`: will the user instantly see themselves in it?
- `Defensibility`: can the team explain why this beat is true?
- `Visual distinctness`: does it deserve its own treatment?
- `Share potential`: would anyone post this?
- `Bridge value`: does it hand off into a next action or persistent artifact?

Kill or combine beats that are low on novelty or defensibility.

## Good Default Arc

For most recap systems:
1. broad anchor
2. identity or habit
3. evolution
4. remarkable moment
5. proof of work
6. optional deeper beat
7. shareable summary

## Anti-Patterns

- Three cards in a row that all say “you did a lot”
- Identity cards before the user trusts the data
- Comparison cards without a clear reference class
- Deep weird cards before the user has context
- A share card that introduces metrics the run never explained
