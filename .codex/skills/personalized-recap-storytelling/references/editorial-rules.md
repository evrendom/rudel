# Editorial Rules

This file governs voice, labels, and anti-cringe discipline.

## Voice

Aim for:
- specific
- concise
- lightly playful
- emotionally legible
- evidence-backed

Avoid:
- fake friendship
- therapy-speak
- generic hype
- product chest-thumping
- overclaiming what the data means

## Good Beat Copy Shape

Most strong beats have:
- one line that makes the claim
- one line that proves it

Example structure:
- `Claim`: You kept shipping.
- `Proof`: 62% of your sessions ended with a commit.

## Identity Packaging

Keep these separate:
- `Descriptive archetype`: a label grounded in behavior
- `Character packaging`: the more playful wrapper
- `Community role`: the user’s place inside a larger group

Do not mix them into one muddy label.

## Naming Rules

- Use concrete language before clever language.
- Name the behavior, not the aspiration.
- Prefer labels that sound like something a user might repeat.
- If a label needs a paragraph of explanation, it is too weak.

## Anti-Cringe Checks

Cut or rewrite copy that:
- sounds like brand campaign filler
- flatters every user the same way
- makes intimate claims the data cannot support
- uses irony as a substitute for insight
- feels like it was generated without taste

## Restraint

Not every beat needs a metaphor.
Not every card needs a punchline.
Some of the strongest recap copy is plain, sharp, and quietly confident.
