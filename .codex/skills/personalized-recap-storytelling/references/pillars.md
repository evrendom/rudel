# Pillars

Use these pillars as a completeness check before you design individual cards.

## 1. Ritual

Why does this recap exist now?
Define the cadence, anticipation, and cultural or product moment it is trying to own.

## 2. Story

What is the emotional arc?
Choose the beat sequence, pacing, escalation, and ending rather than treating the recap like a stat dump.

## 3. Trust

Why should the user believe it?
Every beat needs clear methodology, clear filters, and a recovery path when the result surprises the user.

## 4. Social

Why would anyone share it?
Design for public meaning, not just private delight. The shared artifact should make the user look smart, intense, funny, impressive, or self-aware immediately.

## 5. Operations

Can it survive launch?
Eligibility rules, sparse-data handling, localization, QA, rollout timing, and launch metrics are part of the storytelling system.

## 6. Afterlife

What remains after the run ends?
Decide what persists: hub, playlist, badge, saved report, replay surface, next-step recommendation, or export.

## 7. Control

How much control does the user have?
Define rewind, skip, replay, pace, resume, and re-entry behavior up front.

## 8. Localization

Will this still work outside the default market?
Check language expansion, cultural portability, visual density, and edge cases.

## 9. Evaluation

How do you know it is good?
Use a rubric before shipping. Do not rely on “it feels Wrapped-like.”
