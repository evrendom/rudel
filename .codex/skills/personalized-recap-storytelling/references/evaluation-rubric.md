# Evaluation Rubric

Score each category from 1 to 5 before shipping.

## 1. Frame

Is the protagonist clear?
Does the recap know what moment it is marking?

## 2. Beat Quality

Does each beat add a new lens?
Are there any redundant or weak cards?

## 3. Trust

Can every result be defended?
Are filters, cutoffs, and reference classes clear internally?

## 4. Editorial Quality

Is the copy sharp, specific, and restrained?
Do identity cards feel earned instead of forced?

## 5. Shareability

Is there an obvious artifact a user would actually post?
Does the artifact carry meaning without extra explanation?

## 6. Control And Recovery

Can users replay, revisit, or recover if they miss something?

## 7. Sparse-Data Safety

Does the recap still work for low-data users?
Are fallbacks graceful?

## 8. Localization Safety

Will this still work across languages, markets, and odd usage patterns?

## Ship Criteria

Do not ship if:
- trust is weak
- sparse-data behavior is broken
- the share artifact is unclear
- the run has obvious filler

If two beats compete, keep the one with stronger self-recognition and clearer public meaning.
