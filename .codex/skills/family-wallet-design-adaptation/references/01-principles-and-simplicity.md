# Principles And Simplicity

Family's design system in the article is built around three ideas:

- simplicity
- fluidity
- delight

These are decision rules, not decorative themes.

## Simplicity

- Keep the product approachable.
- Do not present every capability at once.
- Reveal complexity as it becomes relevant.
- Make high-friction flows feel manageable.
- Prefer one clear action or one clear piece of information at a time.

## Gradual revelation

- Do not expose all options simultaneously.
- Keep each step focused on one primary action or one primary piece of information.
- Prefer shorter, more approachable sequences over dense all-in-one surfaces.
- Rewrite content or restructure the screen when a transition or next step is unclear.

## Product implications

- Onboarding-like moments should not feel intimidating.
- Confirmation states should feel obvious and lightweight.
- Supporting information should stay quiet until it becomes necessary.

## Translation for this repo

- The route should feel like a mobile product, not a dashboard set piece.
- Clarity comes before style.
- If a section tries to explain too much at once, split it.
- Replace technical framing with welcoming product framing.
